StoneShooter

Important Note
- The shipped playable page in this folder is index.html plus the external runtime in playable.js.
- That page is titled "Trio Breaker: Puzzle Versus."
- The old inline script in index.html is now kept as a disabled legacy snapshot, while playable.js is the active source of truth.

Type
- Arcade puzzle action game with solo play and local versus play.
- The board is based on column picking rather than free swapping or falling-piece placement.

Main Rule Set
- Stones are stacked in columns.
- You can only take the bottom-most stone from a column.
- The goal is to collect exactly three stones of the same color in a row to clear a set.
- Once you take one color, your active chain is locked to that color until you either finish the triplet or break the chain.
- If you finish the triplet, the stones clear, combo state rises, and versus garbage can be sent.
- If you break the color sequence, the chain resets and you lose combo momentum.

Modes
- Solo Practice creates one board and measures your clear time.
- Local Versus supports 2P, 3P, and 4P.
- Versus is round-based. The winner of a round scores a point.
- A full versus match ends when someone reaches 5 points.

Board And Match Structure
- The board uses 8 columns.
- The current playable inline version uses 10 rows.
- Each round starts with a randomized puzzle board.
- In versus, each active player gets their own board.
- The HUD shows either solo timer information or versus score information depending on mode.

Controls
- Player 1:
- Move cursor left/right with A and D or the left/right arrow keys.
- Pick the current bottom stone with Space or Enter.
- Reset your current board state with R.
- Player 2:
- Move with J and L.
- Pick with Right Shift or Numpad 0.
- Reset with U.
- Gamepads are also polled. Standard D-pad or left stick can move, A confirms, B resets, and Start pauses.
- P pauses.
- M returns to menu from an active match.
- G toggles the debug panel.

Versus Mechanics
- Clearing a triplet sends garbage to another board.
- Garbage is inserted as a blocking row rather than a normal color-clear piece.
- The center HUD shows the running round score for every joined player.
- If one player clears their board first, the round ends after a short delay and either advances to the next round or ends the full match at 5 points.

Combos And Pressure
- Combo state exists and is time-based.
- Successive clears inside the combo window build combo count.
- Combo pressure can send more garbage and rewards efficient route planning instead of random picking.
- The board also supports garbage queues and impact-style feedback such as flash, hit stop, and sound cues.

UI Flow
- Menu screen supports Solo Practice, 2P Versus, 3P Versus, 4P Versus, and How to Play.
- Pause screen supports resume, restart, and quit to menu.
- Game-over screen supports replay and menu return.
- The page uses one canvas plus overlay menus instead of separate screens or route changes.

Audio And Feedback
- The game uses Web Audio API tones instead of external music tracks.
- Movement, pickup, garbage, and other actions all trigger simple synthesized sound effects.
- The page also uses screen flash, colorful animated menu backgrounds, and board-side visual feedback for clears and impacts.

Win And Loss Conditions
- In solo, you win by clearing the board.
- In solo, failing to resolve the board or filling the danger state can end the run.
- In versus, a round win comes from clearing your board before opponents do.
- The match winner is the first player to 5 round wins.
