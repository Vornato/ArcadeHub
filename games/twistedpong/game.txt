Twisted Pong

Type
- Neon-styled arena pong game with local multiplayer, bots, power-ups, campaign progression, and boss encounters.
- The game is a single self-contained HTML page with canvas rendering, menu overlays, procedural audio, and local save data.

Core Loop
- Start from the animated menu, choose a mode, then set player count and player slots.
- Launch a match where paddles defend arena sides while one or more balls ricochet through the field.
- Survive, score, or eliminate rivals depending on mode.
- Collect power-ups during live play.
- Finish the match, unlock cosmetics or progression rewards, and continue through arcade or campaign structure.

Main Modes
- Versus: custom local multiplayer with human and bot slot setup.
- Chaos: much higher power-up density and more unpredictable ball states.
- Survival: last paddle standing wins.
- Boss Rush: fight special boss-controlled paddles.
- Campaign: chapter-based progression with locked and unlockable stages.
- Arcade progression also exists through the stage list and moves forward if Player 1 wins.

Player Counts
- Supports 2, 3, and 4 active players.
- When fewer than four real competitors are active, wall paddles are inserted on missing sides to preserve the arena shape.
- Paddles can occupy left, right, top, and bottom sides depending on player count.

Match Structure
- Balls start from the arena center and bounce off paddles and walls.
- If a ball exits through a player's defended side, that side loses a life.
- The game checks for last-survivor conditions and resolves the winner after a short delay.
- Arcade and campaign progression can automatically move to the next stage or chapter after a win.

Stages And Campaign
- Arcade stages currently include Neon Valley, Signal Drift, Glitch Core, Void Pulse, and System Burn.
- These stages alter background color, speed pressure, and glitch effects.
- Campaign chapters vary player count and can inject boss fights like Sentinel Wall and Void Mirror.
- Campaign progress is saved in localStorage and used to unlock later chapters from the map screen.

Power-Ups
- Current random power-up pool includes:
- Multi Ball
- Giant Paddle
- Tiny Paddle Curse
- Speed Burst
- Slow Time
- Ghost Ball
- Reverse Controls
- Shield
- Clone Ball
- Power Catch
- Power-ups can be instant, timed owner buffs, or timed curses applied to opponents.
- Chaos mode increases power-up spawn frequency.

Power Catch System
- Power Catch is more advanced than a normal hit modifier.
- A paddle with the effect can trap an incoming ball, charge the shot, sweep the aim angle, and release a stronger manual launch.
- The game renders a held-ball arrow and a charge display for that state.

Bosses
- Boss Rush and some campaign chapters can replace a normal side with a boss paddle.
- Implemented boss types include Sentinel Wall, Glitch Hydra, and Void Mirror.
- Bosses modify paddle size, speed, effects, and special behavior instead of being simple health sponges.

Bots And Slot Setup
- Setup screen allows human and bot slot assignment.
- Bots have levels and use side-aware movement plus ball prediction with deliberate error based on skill.
- The gamepad status bar and slot setup screen show who is connected and which side they will control.

Controls
- Menu navigation supports keyboard and gamepad.
- Gamepads can move through menu focus, confirm options, and drive paddle movement in matches.
- Keyboard charge bindings exist per side for held-shot behavior.
- The game also supports standard movement and action bindings per side for keyboard users.

Presentation And Audio
- The game uses a heavy neon/CRT visual theme with animated menu panels.
- Arena grid backgrounds, trails, glitch flashes, floating text, and HUD overlays are all rendered directly on canvas.
- Audio is generated procedurally with the Web Audio API.
- Separate music states exist for menu, normal play, and intense play.
- Music intensity can react to life totals and boss situations.

Persistence
- Local storage saves stats, unlockable skins, achievements, and campaign progress.
- Unlock checks currently reward skins after enough wins, power-up pickups, or boss victories.

What Works Today

- 2P, 3P, and 4P arena logic.

- Arcade stage progression.

Current Scope
- This is much more than a basic pong clone.
- The implemented feature set makes it a party arena game with progression systems, boss encounters, unlocks, and mode-specific rule sets layered onto the core paddle-and-ball format.
