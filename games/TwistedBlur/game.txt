TwistedBlur

Type
- Local multiplayer combat racing game built on an HTML5 canvas.
- Supports split-screen play, AI drivers, pickups, arcade vehicle physics, destructible props, and multiple match types.

Important Current State
- The code is ahead of the README.
- The current implementation defines 10 playable vehicles, 7 levels, and 5 match modes.
- One of the active modes is Drift Attack, which replaces the older "4 modes only" description in the README.

Core Loop
- Move from the title screen into the lobby flow.
- Join human players through keyboard schemes or gamepads.
- Choose each participant's vehicle.
- Pick a mode and a compatible level.
- Start the match and either race, fight, drift for score, or simply survive depending on the mode.
- Use boost, the basic gun, and pickup-based special weapons while navigating hazards, ramps, and off-road penalties.

Player Counts And Views
- Supports 1 to 4 local human players.
- Split-screen layouts adapt to player count:
- 1 player uses a full-screen camera.
- 2 players use a side-by-side or stacked split depending on aspect.
- 3 players use a wide top view plus two lower panes.
- 4 players use four quadrants.
- AI fill is supported and bot difficulty can be adjusted.

Current Match Modes
- Combat Race: three-lap race with combat, checkpoints, respawns, and a finish ranking.
- Arena Deathmatch: kill-target or timer-based arena combat with respawns.
- Survival: no-respawn elimination mode where survival time decides the result.
- Drift Attack: bank drift score and near-miss style momentum before the timer expires.
- Quick Battle: short, fast party-combat round with kills and time pressure.

Driving And Physics
- Vehicles use an arcade handling model instead of rigid-body simulation.
- Core systems include throttle, braking, steering, traction, drift state, boost usage, airborne time, ramp jumps, body roll, body pitch, and landing handling.
- Surface sampling changes traction and speed based on road, off-road, slow zones, and boost pads.
- Race-like modes use checkpoints and lap counters.
- Respawn logic places vehicles back near recent checkpoints or proper arena spawn points.

Combat And Pickups
- Every vehicle has a base firing system plus a pickup-driven special weapon slot.
- Pickup and weapon definitions currently include Rockets, Homing Missile, Mines, EMP Burst, Shockwave, Shield, Rail Lance, Flak Burst, Arc Net, and Repair.
- The weapon system handles projectile travel, mines, homing logic, EMP shocks, shield activation, line weapons, splash effects, and prop damage.
- Pickups are spawned from weighted tables and level-specific pickup points.

Hazards And Track Features
- Levels can contain boost pads, ramps, shortcut paths, pulse hazards, laser walls, damage zones, sand or slow zones, and static obstacles.
- Destructible props are placed per level and feed into weapon hit logic and effect systems.
- Arena levels are more combat-dense, while race levels emphasize checkpoints, routes, and shortcut risk.

Bots
- Three bot difficulty presets exist: Rookie, Street, and Ace.
- Bot AI changes throttle quality, aim quality, aggression, recovery, chase logic, and boost behavior.
- Bots choose between racing lines, pickups, attacks, and survival behavior based on mode.

Scoring And Win Logic
- Combat Race finishes by lap completion and race placement.
- Arena Deathmatch and Quick Battle resolve from kill target or timer.
- Survival resolves by last survivor or best survival time.
- Drift Attack banks drift score, combo value, and clean-exit bonuses before the timer ends.
- Match results are rendered through a dedicated results phase, not a one-line alert.

Controls
- Gamepad driving includes steer, throttle, brake/reverse, fire, special, boost, look-back, and pause.
- Multiple keyboard schemes are implemented for local testing so several humans can play on one machine.
- Pause state, results flow, and menu navigation are all handled in the same codebase.

Presentation
- Each human gets a dedicated camera and HUD.
- The UI handles title screen, lobby, pause screen, and results.
- Minimap framing, announcer messaging, speed effects, smoke, sparks, shockwaves, and explosions are implemented.
- Audio includes placeholder synth music plus engine-state and SFX hooks.
